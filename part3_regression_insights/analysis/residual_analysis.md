# Residual Analysis

## Residual Calculation

Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales

Positive residuals indicate actual sales were higher than predicted.

Negative residuals indicate actual sales were lower than predicted.

## Largest Positive Residuals

The largest positive residuals represent stores that significantly outperformed model expectations.

Possible reasons:

* Exceptional local demand
* Strong store management
* Special promotions
* Favorable location factors not included in the model

## Largest Negative Residuals

The largest negative residuals represent stores that underperformed relative to expectations.

Possible reasons:

* Local competition
* Operational issues
* Temporary disruptions
* Customer satisfaction problems

## Business Interpretation

The model predicts most stores reasonably well but some stores perform substantially above or below expectations.

This suggests additional variables may influence sales that are not included in the model.

## Under-Prediction and Over-Prediction

Some high-performing stores appear under-predicted, while some low-performing stores appear over-predicted.

This indicates that factors beyond marketing, footfall, inventory availability, and store type affect monthly sales.
