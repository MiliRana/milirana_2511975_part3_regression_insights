# Retail Sales Regression Analysis

## Business Problem Summary

The objective of this project is to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales across retail stores. Regression analysis was used to evaluate how store characteristics and operational variables influence sales performance and to provide business recommendations based on data-driven insights.

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains information for 320 retail stores.

Key variables include:

* monthly_sales (Target Variable)
* marketing_spend
* footfall
* inventory_availability_pct
* store_size_sqft
* employee_count
* store_location
* store_age_years

The dataset was cleaned before analysis to remove inconsistencies and prepare variables for regression modeling.

---

## Dependent Variable

* monthly_sales

This variable represents the monthly sales generated by each store.

---

## Independent Variables

Variables evaluated during regression analysis:

* marketing_spend
* footfall
* inventory_availability_pct
* store_size_sqft
* employee_count
* store_age_years
* store_location (converted into dummy variables)

---

## Regression Approach

The project used both simple and multiple linear regression.

### Simple Regression Models

Model 1:

* monthly_sales ~ marketing_spend

Model 2:

* monthly_sales ~ footfall

### Multiple Regression Model

monthly_sales was modeled using:

* marketing_spend
* footfall
* inventory_availability_pct
* Store_Mall (dummy variable)

---

## Dummy Variable Approach

The categorical variable store_location was converted into dummy variables.

Dummy variable used:

* Store_Mall

Reference category:

* Residential

Interpretation:

A positive coefficient for Store_Mall indicates that mall stores are associated with higher monthly sales compared to residential stores.

---

## Model Comparison Summary

| Model                     | R-Squared |
| ------------------------- | --------- |
| Marketing Spend Model     | 0.167     |
| Footfall Model            | 0.736     |
| Multiple Regression Model | 0.809     |

### Key Findings

* Marketing spend has a positive relationship with sales.
* Footfall has a strong positive relationship with sales.
* Inventory availability significantly impacts sales.
* Mall stores tend to generate higher sales than residential stores.
* The multiple regression model provides the best explanatory power.

---

## Final Model Selected

Multiple Regression Model

Variables Included:

* marketing_spend
* footfall
* inventory_availability_pct
* Store_Mall

Reason for Selection:

* Highest R-squared value (0.809)
* All variables statistically significant
* Strong business interpretability
* Better predictive performance than simple regression models

---

## Business Recommendation

Based on the regression analysis:

1. Increase customer footfall through marketing and promotional activities.
2. Maintain high inventory availability to avoid stock-outs.
3. Continue investing in effective marketing initiatives.
4. Prioritize high-performing store locations and formats.
5. Use regression insights to support future operational and expansion decisions.

---

## Assumptions and Limitations

### Assumptions

* Relationships between variables are linear.
* Errors are independent.
* Variance of residuals remains relatively constant.
* Variables are measured accurately.

### Limitations

* Regression shows association, not causation.
* Seasonal effects were not included.
* Competitor actions were not included.
* Customer demographics were not included.
* External economic conditions were not included.

## Conclusion

The analysis shows that footfall, inventory availability, marketing spend, and store location are important factors associated with monthly sales. The multiple regression model explains approximately 81% of the variation in sales and provides the strongest support for business decision-making.
