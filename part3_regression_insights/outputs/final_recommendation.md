# Final Recommendation

## Key Factors Associated with Monthly Sales

The analysis indicates that the strongest factors associated with monthly sales are:

1. Footfall
2. Inventory Availability
3. Marketing Spend
4. Store Location Type (Mall)

Footfall showed the strongest relationship with sales.

## Areas Leadership Should Focus On

* Increasing store traffic
* Maintaining high inventory availability
* Optimizing marketing investments
* Expanding successful store formats

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Regression results show association rather than proof of causation.

Store type and marketing spend may be influenced by other business conditions not included in the model.

## Recommended Business Actions

* Improve customer acquisition initiatives.
* Reduce stock shortages.
* Allocate marketing budgets toward high-performing locations.
* Monitor store performance using data-driven metrics.

## Risks and Limitations

* External economic factors were not included.
* Customer demographics were not included.
* Competitor actions were not included.
* Seasonal effects may influence results.

## Association vs Causation

Regression identifies statistical relationships between variables.

A significant coefficient does not automatically prove that changing a variable will directly cause sales to increase.

Additional testing and business validation are required before making strategic decisions.
